<p>Frilya Rachma Putri is a senior lecturer in the Department of Psychiatry
at Universitas Brawijaya , Malang, East Java, Indonesia. She is a child
and adolescent psychiatrist from Indonesia who has a passion for studying
child &amp; adolescent mental health, developmental psychiatry, and psychotherapy
especially in children and women. She works at the Faculty of Medicine,
Universitas Brawijaya, a state university to teach medical students about
psychiatry and mental health to improve the mental health status in Indonesia,
especially in Malang. She is also a member of the Health Research Ethics
Commission of Universitas Brawijaya. She is one of the local surveyors
of The Forum for Ethical Review Committees in the Asian and Western Pacific
Region (FERCAP). She is also a member of a Neuroscience study group and
a Social Medicine study group at the Faculty of Medicine, Universitas&nbsp;Brawijaya.
She empowered communities by providing education at the schools in Malang
to increase mental resilience, especially in adolescents.</p>
