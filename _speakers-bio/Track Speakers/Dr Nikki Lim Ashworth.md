<p>Dr. Nikki Lim-Ashworth is a Senior Clinical Psychologist at the Child
Guidance Clinic, Institute of Mental Health. She was awarded the Ministry
of Health NMRC Training Fellowship and earned her Doctorate in Clinical
Psychology from University College London, UK, in 2016. She is a Chartered
Clinical Psychologist registered with the British Health and Care Professions
Council. With over a decade of experience, Dr. Lim-Ashworth specialises
in working with children and adolescents facing a range of mental health
challenges. She has a particular expertise in ADHD, providing support not
only to young individuals but also to their families. Her clinical interests
focus on evidence-based treatments for childhood ADHD and their practical
application in healthcare settings. Dr Lim-Ashworth has co-authored several
research papers on ADHD and child mental health, as well as contributed
to numerous research studies, including large-scale clinical trials investigating
the effects of omega-3 supplementation and neurofeedback interventions
on ADHD outcomes. Currently, she serves as one of the co-leads in the revision
of the Singapore Clinical Practice Guidelines for ADHD. Beyond clinical
work, Dr. Lim-Ashworth is actively involved in tertiary academic teaching,
educating undergraduates in medicine and psychology.</p>

<p><strong>Synopsis</strong>
<br>The talk will examine ADHD care starting with essential statistics from
a local specialist ADHD service to highlight significant trends including
the profiles, functioning, and parenting stressors among young persons
with ADHD and their families, and treatment outcomes. Further review will
be done on the assessment and intervention available to adults with ADHD
in Singapore. The talk will round off with a discussion on the emerging
best practices shaping ADHD care in young persons and adults.</p>
